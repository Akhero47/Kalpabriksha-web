  • 2079, Yearly Recap

This year marked the first year of Kalpabriksha's operations, and we are thrilled to have made significant progress towards our goal of promoting growth and support for Nepalese youth and communities. We are grateful for the support of our volunteers, partners, and stakeholders who have worked with us to make a positive impact.


Change: Because We Can was one of the flagship programs of Kalpabriksha this year, and we are proud to have impacted over 300 students in 7+ schools with our interactive and effective learning methods. We plan to expand this program further in the coming years to reach even more students and promote awareness on sensitive issues.


History: Gaze at the Maze was another program that we did this year, which aimed to promote authentic research on Nepalese history. The program was well-received, and we look forward to organizing similar initiatives that promote intellectual growth and development.


Yuwantar, our first annual youth carnival, was a success, with young people from different parts of the valley coming together to promote collective development and emotional well-being. The Proudly Queering session and collaboration with Girls Empowered by Travel-GET for the "16 Days of Activism" campaign were also highlights of our work this year.


As we look back on our first year, we are humbled by the support and encouragement that we have received. We remain committed to our principles of equity, inclusion, and youth innovation and will continue to work towards creating positive change in Nepalese society. We would like to express our gratitude to everyone who has supported us in our journey so far and look forward to continued collaboration in the years to come.
